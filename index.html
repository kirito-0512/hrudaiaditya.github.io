About
Me

I grew up genuinely curious about how things learn. Not learn in the rote sense, but learn the way a child figures out physics by throwing things off tables. That curiosity pulled me through an Honours in AI/ML at Vasavi College of Engineering and eventually into building production AI systems at scale.

Right now I work on Infosys Agentic Foundry one of the more interesting engineering problems I've encountered. We're not just wrapping APIs; we're figuring out how to give agents real memory, real tools, and the ability to reason across multi-step enterprise workflows without hallucinating on the parts that matter. Getting accuracy from 76% to 91% across three domains in months taught me more about evaluation design than any course could.

Outside of work, I have a weird obsession with reinforcement learning fine-tuning specifically what happens when you try to teach LLMs through environment feedback rather than human labels. I've been equally fascinated by evolutionary algorithms ever since I realised that natural selection is basically gradient-free optimisation running for 3.8 billion years. I keep thinking there's something underexplored in hybrid evolutionary-RL approaches for sparse reward problems.

And then there's astrophysics my completely impractical love. I find the timescales and scales involved genuinely mind-bending. There's something clarifying about realising your billion-parameter model is running on a pale blue dot orbiting an unremarkable star. I read papers on star formation, dark matter simulations, and gravitational wave detection the same way I read ML papers: looking for the surprising idea buried in the methodology.

I believe the best engineers are the ones who read widely outside their field. A paper on ant colony behaviour probably has more to teach us about distributed systems than most distributed systems papers. That's the instinct behind my weekly research habit.

Featured Build

Know Agent
Builder

This one started as a personal frustration. Every time I wanted to experiment with a new agent architecture, I'd spend three days wiring up infrastructure before writing a single line of actual agent logic. So I built the platform I wished existed a fully containerised environment where you can go from idea to running multi-agent workflow in minutes, not days.

The real design challenge was observability. Agents fail in ways that are invisible if you're not looking. I integrated OpenTelemetry end-to-end, piped everything into Elasticsearch, and built Grafana dashboards that actually tell you whether your agent is reasoning or just confidently hallucinating. Arize Phoenix handles the LLM evaluation side so you can compare runs, spot regression, and understand exactly where a chain of thought went wrong.
